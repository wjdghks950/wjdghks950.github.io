About

Ph.D. candidate at BLENDER Lab at University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), currently advised by Professor Heng Ji

I obtained my Master's from the School of Computing, KAIST, where I was advised by Prof. Sung-Hyon Myaeng.

My primary research interests lie in the intersection of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Learning (ML). I want to build next-generation language models that are capable of dealing with the interplay of general and domain-specific knowledge; the knowledge, either parametric or non-paramaeteric, could also manifest in various modalities, e.g., images, which has recently motivated me to look into multimodal large language models (MLLMs).

Currently, I'm interested in developing multimodal large language models (MLLMs) that can process both the visual and textual modalities, and understand them in the light of parametric and external knowledge.

Here are some keywords about my research interests:

  • Multimodal Large Language Models (e.g., Large Vision Language Models)
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) & Language Modeling
  • Question Answering (QA) using LLMs
